# Bubbles and narration

- Dialogue belongs to an identified speaker and expresses character intent.
- Narration supplies source-grounded context that action cannot show cleanly.
- Do not repeat the same fact in dialogue, narration, and caption.
- Keep bubble order visually obvious and copy concise enough for safe zones.
- Never fabricate a direct quotation from paraphrased source material.
- Sound effects support action and tone; they do not carry required facts.
